UNENDING LOGICAL ACTUALISM IS A LOGIC SYSTEM THAT CAN BE SUCCESFULLY USED IN PROGRAMMING LANGUAGES
These papers proposes a paradigmatic shift in contemporary metamathematical logic by confronting the stasis of the Gödelian system with the dynamics of Infinite-Logical Actualism. The central argument maintains that Gödel’s Incompleteness Theorem operates under the premise of zero processing time (t = 0), resulting in a static view of truth. In contrast, we introduce the temporal variable (t > 0) and the Update Operator (⊕), redefining the systemic structure as a processual function (Sₜ₊₁ = Sₜ ⊕ α ∙ ΔIₜ). Through the Axiom of Epistemic Stratification, truth is deconstructed into three fundamental strata: Evident Truth (Vᴇ), Probable Truth (Vᴘᴩ), and Possible Truth (Vᴘₒ). It is demonstrated that the classical "undecidability" of Gödel's sentence G does not constitute an intrinsic failure of reason, but rather a processing latency in a state of Potency. The transition from potential to act is mediated by the Agency Vector (α), whose intellectual pressure over the Potency Gradient (∇Vᴘₒ) enables the continuous expansion of the system. Prado’s Convergence Theorem establishes that, in the asymptotic limit of infinite time (t → ∞), the sum of successive updates tends toward total completeness (Ꝟₜₒₜₐₗ). It is concluded that the logical system is not limited by syntactic gaps, but is instead characterized as a perpetually expanding horizon, where incompleteness is merely the time required for the manifestation of agency over the infinite.
